Time Left clock
work: concept, design,
electronics, software, assembly

You've heard the adage "time is money" right? Me too. The goal of this clock is to make us think of time as a resource, and to keep track of how much we have left to spend. To do this, it presents five familiar circular clock faces displaying unfamiliar units of time—a month, a week, a year, etc. The "100%" at the top indicates to a new viewer how those units of time are being measured. This was a team project.

Pixels cereal
work: concept, visual design,
packaging, baking cereal

Wouldn't it be cool if you could eat color? More specifically, what if you could eat a digital construct: a pixel. That's the idea here. Instead of eating strawberry or chocolate cereal, you could have green or RGB or CMYK. The package is inteded to communicate the idea to a viewer, and inside was actual cereal I prototyped. The concept was provoked when I heard about the New Aesthetic art movement.

DueCourse.org
work: concept, visual design,
software, marketing

How does Wharton decide what new courses to offer? After meeting with almost a dozen professors I discovered that they have to guess what classes students will want to enroll for. This site allows students and professors to post ideas, and vote on classes they'd be interested in taking. Check out some of the ideas on the site.
